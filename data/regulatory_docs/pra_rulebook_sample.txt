# PRA Rulebook - Capital Requirements (Simplified Sample)

## Chapter 1: Own Funds

### 1.1 Common Equity Tier 1 Capital

Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1) capital consists of the following items:

1.1.1 Capital instruments provided they meet all the following criteria:
(a) The instruments are issued directly by the institution with the prior approval of the owners of the institution
(b) The instruments are paid up and their purchase is not funded directly or indirectly by the institution
(c) The instruments meet all the criteria for classification as equity under applicable accounting standards
(d) The instruments are perpetual
(e) The principal amount of the instruments may not be reduced or repaid except in liquidation

1.1.2 Share premium accounts relating to instruments included in CET1 capital

1.1.3 Retained earnings, including interim profits that have been verified by independent auditors

1.1.4 Accumulated other comprehensive income and other disclosed reserves

1.1.5 Other items approved by the PRA as CET1 capital

### 1.2 Additional Tier 1 Capital

Additional Tier 1 (AT1) capital consists of the following items:

1.2.1 Capital instruments that meet all the following conditions:
(a) The instruments are issued directly by the institution
(b) The instruments are subordinated, perpetual, and fully paid up
(c) The instruments are not secured or covered by a guarantee that enhances the seniority of the claim
(d) The instruments include a provision for principal loss absorption through either conversion to CET1 or a write-down mechanism

1.2.2 Share premium accounts relating to instruments included in AT1 capital

### 1.3 Tier 2 Capital

Tier 2 capital consists of the following items:

1.3.1 Capital instruments and subordinated loans that meet all the following conditions:
(a) The instruments are subordinated to depositors and general creditors
(b) The instruments have an original maturity of at least five years
(c) The instruments do not contain incentives to redeem

1.3.2 Share premium accounts relating to instruments included in Tier 2 capital

1.3.3 General credit risk adjustments (for institutions using the IRB approach)

## Chapter 2: Deductions from Own Funds

### 2.1 Deductions from CET1 Capital

Institutions must deduct the following items from CET1 capital:

2.1.1 Losses for the current financial year
2.1.2 Intangible assets, including goodwill
2.1.3 Deferred tax assets that rely on future profitability
2.1.4 Negative amounts resulting from the calculation of expected loss amounts
2.1.5 Direct, indirect and synthetic holdings by the institution of own CET1 instruments

### 2.2 Deductions from AT1 Capital

Institutions must deduct the following items from AT1 capital:

2.2.1 Direct, indirect and synthetic holdings by the institution of own AT1 instruments
2.2.2 Holdings of AT1 instruments of financial sector entities where the institution has a significant investment

### 2.3 Deductions from Tier 2 Capital

Institutions must deduct the following items from Tier 2 capital:

2.3.1 Direct, indirect and synthetic holdings by the institution of own Tier 2 instruments
2.3.2 Holdings of Tier 2 instruments of financial sector entities where the institution has a significant investment

## Chapter 3: Calculation of Own Funds

### 3.1 Total Own Funds Calculation

Total own funds is calculated as:
Total Own Funds = CET1 Capital + AT1 Capital + Tier 2 Capital

Where each tier is calculated after applying the relevant deductions specified in Chapter 2.

### 3.2 Minimum Capital Requirements

Institutions must maintain:
- CET1 capital ratio of at least 4.5% of total risk-weighted assets
- Tier 1 capital ratio (CET1 + AT1) of at least 6% of total risk-weighted assets
- Total capital ratio of at least 8% of total risk-weighted assets
